The British Computer Society (BCS) is an important organisation in the UK for technology. This group started in 1957. They have been important in shaping computing and technology in the UK and other places. The BCS wants to:

  • Advance knowledge
  • Promote professionalism
  • Support IT professionals

They have helped the tech industry grow. This introduction gives an overview of the BCS.

Digital Literacy and Professionalism

BCS is one of the most important organisations in the UK when it comes to teaching people how to use technology and keeping professional standards high. It helps people learn how to use technology by taking part in educational programmes that help people at all stages of schooling get better at IT. The society aims to improve the IT workforce by offering a wide range of:

  • Certifications
  • Qualifications
  • Opportunities for professional growth

These courses:

  • Not only help IT workers improve their skills
  • But they also set industry-wide standards for professionalism and skill

Through its Code of Conduct, BCS makes sure that its members work in a way that is:

  • Honest
  • Responsible
  • Professional

BCS Memberships

  1. Student Membership: This membership is for students who are studying IT-related subjects. It gives them access to: 
    • Honest
    • Responsible
    • Professional

  2. Associate membership is great for people who are: 
    • Just starting out in their careers
    • Moving into the IT field

  3. Member (MBCS) and Chartered IT Professional (CITP) are levels of membership for IT workers with a lot of experience. Members of MBCS have access to more advanced resources, and CITP status shows a better level of knowledge and skill, which is good for a career.

  4. Fellowship (FBCS) Membership: Those who have made important advances to the IT field are given the highest level of membership, FBCS. Fellows get special access and chances to help change the industry.

  5. Retired Membership: This membership is for IT professionals who have retired and 
    • Want to stay in touch with the tech community
    • Get access to tools
    • Keep working in the field

  6. Affiliate Membership: This membership gives people who are not IT workers but are interested in technology access to educational materials and information about technology.

  7. Corporate Membership: Here, it does offer memberships for businesses and other groups. With these memberships, companies can help their employees improve their skills and become known as leaders in the IT field.


BCS Exam Structure

BCS offers certifications at various levels to suit different career stages and expertise levels. These include:

  • Foundation level
  • Practitioner level
  • Intermediate level
  • Professional level

Each level builds on the previous one, allowing individuals to progress in their careers.

The Journey of a BCS Course Follower in 6 Steps

Step 1: Look around and sign up

Exploration: The trip starts with looking through BCS's full list of courses. Followers of a course can go to the BCS website, look at the courses that are offered, and choose the one that fits with their job goals and interests. Here are few options:

  • BCS Practitioner in Business Analysis Practice Certification
  • BCS Practitioner in Business Analysis Practice Course
  • BCS Practitioner in Business Analysis Practice Exam

Enrolment: Once a person has picked a course, they can sign up for it on the BCS website. Creating an account and giving the necessary information is often required to sign up.

Step 2: Get into the course and study

Access to Resources: BCS gives course participants access to a wide range of resources, such as:

  • Study tools
  • Textbooks
  • Online lectures
  • Practise exams

These tools are made to help you learn as much as possible.

Followers of the course can go through the course material at their own pace. They can:

  • Watch video lectures
  • Read materials
  • Take quizzes and tasks

The flexible learning method works for different ways of learning.

Step 3: Make connections and find a mentor

Online Forums and Communities: BCS often sets up online forums and communities where people who are taking the same course can:

  • Meet other students
  • Share their experiences
  • Ask questions
  • Get help with course-related issues

Mentorship Programmes: Some BCS courses may have mentorship programmes that put people who are taking the course in touch with experienced workers in their field of choice. These mentors give:

  • Guidance
  • Career advice
  • Information

Step 4: Taking a test and getting a certificate

Assessment: At the end of a course, students usually have to do some kind of assessment. This could be:

  • An exam
  • A project
  • A practical task

These tests measure how well they know and understand the course information.
Certification: Course participants get a BCS certification when they finish the course and do well on the tests. This licence shows that they are knowledgeable and skilled in a certain area of IT.

Step 5: Keep learning and growing in your job

Access to Continuing Education: BCS knows that learning is something you do for the rest of your life. After getting a certification, people who follow a course can get access to more learning resources, such as advanced courses or news about their area.
Career Advancement: People who take the course and get BCS certifications and keep learning can feel confident about pursuing career possibilities. Employers value BCS certifications, and they can help you get a raise and make more money at your job.

Step 6: Helping out your community

Contribution: Many people who take BCS courses choose to give back to the tech community by:

  • Joining in forums
  • Mentoring others
  • Even becoming BCS ambassadors

Their knowledge and experience are important to the IT community as a whole.

7 Tips for Exam Takers

  1. Get going early: Start getting ready for your test early enough to give yourself enough time to study and practise. Avoid rushing at the last minute because it can cause stress and make you forget things.
  2. Learn about the exam's goals and content areas. The BCS exam you're taking will have unique goals and content areas. This helps you study the things that are most important.
  3. Make an organised study plan that tells you what you need to cover each day or week. A well-organized plan makes sure that all of the information is covered.
  4. Use Official Study Materials: Materials are made to match the topic and goals of the exam. For your specific exam, use the official BCS:
    • Study materials
    • Textbooks
    • Online tools
  5. Mock tests are a good way to practise. Use the practise exams and mock tests that BCS offer. These make you feel like you're in the exam room, so you can get used to the style and see if you're ready.
  6. Ask for Help: If you come across difficult topics or ideas, don't be afraid to ask for help. Reach out to;
    • Other students
    • Your teacher
    • Online groups
  7. Stay Organised: Keep your notes, resources, and learning materials in order. This makes it easy to find information when you need it and takes some of the stress out of getting ready.

Frequently asked questions

If you're stepping into PRINCE2 without prior qualifications, it's advisable to start with the PRINCE2 7th Edition Foundation and Practitioner Courses

For holders of the PRINCE2 6th Edition Practitioner certification wanting to transition to the 7th Edition, beginning with the 7th Edition Foundation course is recommended.

Foundation Level - The 7th Edition retains the Foundation level examination format. Yet, the passing criteria have been elevated from 55% to 60%, requiring 36 right answers. Practitioner Level - The Practitioner exam for the 7th Edition has expanded to 70 questions, a slight increase from 68 in the previous version. While the exam duration remains at 2.5 hours, the passing score is now 60%. This exam will derive its questions from one of the four new manual scenarios.

The PRINCE2® Foundation (7th Edition) training course will be available starting from September 2023

Foundation Level - No prior qualifications are necessary for the 7th Edition Foundation course.
